# Project Mime: Current Features (V1 Architecture)

Project Mime is an end-to-end AI robotics teleoperation platform. It allows users to control industrial robotic arms using an ultra-low-cost, wireless IMU glove and a standard webcam, completely eliminating the IMU drift problem that plagues $10,000 motion capture suits.

## Core Capabilities

### 1. Aerospace-Grade Sensor Fusion (Zero-Drift)
- **The Problem:** Standard IMU gloves drift over time, making them useless for precise, long-term robotic control.
- **The Mime Solution:** An **Extended Kalman Filter (EKF)** written in high-performance C++. It fuses 100Hz gyroscope/accelerometer data from the ESP32 glove with absolute spatial coordinates generated by a Python MediaPipe Computer Vision daemon analyzing your standard webcam. The camera mathematically "anchors" the IMU, resulting in perfect, drift-free spatial tracking forever.

### 2. Hardware Agnostic Inverse Kinematics (IK)
- The system includes a standalone Python IK Engine powered by **PyBullet**. 
- It ingests the absolute Cartesian coordinates (`[X, Y, Z]`) generated by your hand and instantly runs the complex calculus required to map those coordinates into specific motor joint angles for a physical robot.
- It is completely agnostic: swap out the URDF file, and you can control an $80,000 KUKA arm or a $200 3D-printed desktop arm without changing any code.

### 3. Deep Learning & AI Autonomy Pipeline
- Mime is not just a remote control; it is an AI training platform. 
- A C++ `DataLogger` records mathematically perfect CSV datasets of your physical movements at 100 frames per second.
- Includes a fully built **PyTorch Imitation Learning Pipeline**. It utilizes an **LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory)** neural network to train an AI on your CSV logs, allowing the AI to eventually mimic your physical tasks and generate autonomous robotic trajectories.

### 4. Real-Time WebGL Digital Twin
- A sleek, high-performance React dashboard.
- Uses `urdf-loader` and `Three.js` to render a live 3D "Digital Twin" of the robot arm. 
- The visualizer responds with sub-millisecond latency to your physical hand movements, including mapping the analog flex sensors on your glove to the mechanical fingers of the 3D robot.

### 5. Enterprise Networking Architecture
- Built on **gRPC** and **UDP**.
- Bypasses the bloat and latency of traditional robotics frameworks like ROS, providing an incredibly fast, lightweight, and modular microservice architecture that can run on anything from a high-end desktop to a Raspberry Pi.
